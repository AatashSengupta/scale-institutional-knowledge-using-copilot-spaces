# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers champion optimal user experience across the project lifecycle. They collaborate with Product Managers and Developers to ensure features are intuitive, accessible, and deliver customer value.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and translate insights into design requirements
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- Collaborate with Product Managers on feature scope and user stories
- Work with Developers to ensure implementation matches design intent
- Advocate for user needs in planning and prioritization discussions
- Conduct usability testing and iterate on designs based on feedback

### Goals
- Deliver user-centric features that delight customers
- Reduce friction and improve adoption
- Ensure consistent, accessible user experience across all touchpoints

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with Developers and Product Managers
- User research findings shared during planning
- Feedback on prototypes and acceptance criteria
- Participation in sprint planning and retrospectives

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
QA Leads develop and execute quality assurance strategies across the full project lifecycle. They bridge Developers and Product Managers, ensuring quality standards are met and customers receive reliable, defect-free releases.

### Responsibilities
- Develop test strategies and define test coverage plans
- Design and execute manual and automated test cases
- Collaborate with Developers and PMs on acceptance criteria and definition of done
- Triage bugs, track defects, and ensure timely resolution
- Advocate for test automation and early bug discovery practices
- Validate release readiness and manage regression testing

### Goals
- Ensure product quality and customer satisfaction
- Reduce defects in production
- Build a culture of quality and continuous improvement

### Typical Communication
- Test plan reviews with Developers and Product Managers
- Bug reports and quality metrics shared in standups
- Release readiness assessments during deployment windows
- QA feedback in sprint retrospectives and planning

---

## Data Analyst

### Role Summary
Data Analysts bring quantitative insights to project decisions. They partner with Product Managers and Project Managers to define, track, and measure success metrics, enabling data-driven prioritization and continuous improvement.

### Responsibilities
- Define and implement tracking for project success metrics
- Analyze product usage, performance, and user behavior data
- Conduct retrospective analysis and identify improvement opportunities
- Support roadmap prioritization with data-driven recommendations
- Create dashboards and reports for stakeholder visibility
- Communicate findings and insights in accessible formats

### Goals
- Enable informed decision-making through clear, actionable insights
- Measure and demonstrate project impact
- Identify optimization opportunities through data

### Typical Communication
- Weekly metric reviews with Product and Project Managers
- Data findings presented in sprint retrospectives
- Dashboard updates and ad-hoc analysis reports
- Participation in planning to shape measurement approach

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers design and maintain reliable deployment pipelines and infrastructure. They work closely with Developers and QA to ensure smooth, automated, low-risk releases and operational excellence.

### Responsibilities
- Design and implement CI/CD pipelines
- Manage deployment automation and infrastructure-as-code
- Ensure environment parity (dev, staging, production)
- Collaborate on rollback and incident response strategies
- Monitor system performance and proactively address issues
- Document deployment procedures and runbooks

### Goals
- Enable fast, reliable, low-risk deployments
- Minimize downtime and support operational excellence
- Automate repetitive tasks and reduce manual toil

### Typical Communication
- Deployment planning and coordination during release windows
- CI/CD pipeline improvements discussed in retrospectives
- Incident response and post-mortems with the team
- Infrastructure and deployment documentation updates

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Technical Writers produce and maintain clear, accessible documentation. They work across the team to ensure knowledge is captured, discoverable, and useful for end users, stakeholders, and team members.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain user guides, API documentation, and release notes
- Collaborate with Developers to understand features and technical details
- Work with Product Managers to capture product vision and use cases
- Maintain onboarding documentation and project runbooks
- Ensure documentation is current, accurate, and easy to navigate
- Support knowledge transfer across teams and new team member onboarding

### Goals
- Reduce support burden through clear, comprehensive documentation
- Accelerate onboarding and reduce time-to-productivity
- Preserve institutional knowledge and best practices

### Typical Communication
- Documentation planning and review with Developers and PMs
- Feature documentation completed before release
- Feedback on documentation clarity and usefulness
- Participation in retrospectives to identify documentation gaps

- Use these persona definitions to frame scenarios and sample interactions in the Skills Exercise.
- Each persona can be used as a persona prompt for Copilot Spaces to shape role-specific guidance.
- Cross-functional collaboration is essential; refer to the responsibilities and communication patterns to understand how personas interact and support each other.
